# boil.

Time-blocking with heat. Focus blocks that boil as you work.

---

## What it is

A single HTML file. No build step, no dependencies, no accounts.

Open it in a browser and start.

boil is not about planning your time.

It's about **actually starting**, **staying with**, and **returning to** your work.

---

## What it's for

Most productivity tools optimize for planning.

boil exists to help you **follow through**.

Especially when:
- starting feels hard
- switching tasks breaks momentum
- interruptions derail your day
- consistency collapses after one miss

The goal is simple:

> make it easier to begin, continue, and come back.

---

## How it feels

Your tasks are blocks of time.

Start one and water begins to rise.

As you work, it heats up:
- waves grow
- bubbles form
- the screen simmers
- bioluminescent life drifts beneath the surface

Finish a block and it shatters.

Finish everything and the day is done.

The water is not decoration.

**The water is the feedback.**

---

## How it works

- **Set your day window** — how long you're working today
- **Add blocks** — name, duration, sub-blocks, optional rest
- **Tap to start** — one task runs at a time
- **Water rises continuously** as time passes
- **Blocks complete automatically**
- **Continuous mode** — flow through blocks without stopping
- **Shake to begin** — physically start your day

No dashboards. No scores. No friction.

---

## The water

The water is a living canvas:

- **Wave physics** — a heightfield simulation drives the surface, not canned animation
- **Phone tilt** shifts the water like tilting a glass
- **Touch** creates ripples, pops bubbles, scatters plankton
- **Procedural audio** — water sounds rise and fall with the fill level
- **Anticipatory crescendo** — bubbles, waves, and audio intensify in the final 20% of a block
- **Night sky** — stars appear at night, with rare shooting stars. Tap a star and it twinkles.
- **Bioluminescent ecosystem** — plankton and rare jellyfish-like organisms live in the water, growing as you complete work. They're subtle during focus, vivid between tasks. Touch the water and they scatter, leaving glowing trails.
- **Steam** rises at high heat, glass fogs
- **Day-to-night transitions** — colors, sounds, and energy shift with the time of day

---

## The ecosystem

The water is alive.

Completing work blocks feeds energy into a living ecosystem of bioluminescent plankton. Over days and weeks, the ecosystem grows — more life, brighter glow, and eventually rare soft-bodied organisms drift through.

Stop using the app and the ecosystem gradually enters dormancy. Come back and it rebuilds. It's a garden you tend, not a streak you break.

During a work block, the life is peripheral — subtle dots you notice without watching. Between tasks and during rest, the water comes alive: tap and drag to scatter plankton and leave bioluminescent trails.

No biodiversity score. No creature count. Just life.

---

## The game (secondary)

There are rewards:
- coins per completed block
- stars for achievements
- streaks for consistency
- badges for milestones
- heat levels that grow over your lifetime
- a shop with floaters (rubber duck, paper boat, beach ball), themes, and aquarium mode

These exist to support momentum.

They are not the goal.

If they ever get in the way of real work, they are wrong.

---

## What success looks like

- you start tasks faster
- you stay with them longer
- you come back after interruption
- you finish what actually matters

Not:
- perfect streaks
- perfect blocks
- perfect days

---

## Features

- Works offline as a PWA — add to home screen
- Single-file app (`boil.html`), ~5000 lines, everything included
- Canvas water engine with heightfield wave physics
- Procedural water audio, completion sounds, haptic feedback
- Bioluminescent tide pool ecosystem that grows with your work
- Night sky with stars, shooting stars, and star reflections on water
- Day-to-night color and audio transitions
- Phone tilt affects the water surface
- Touch ripples, bubble popping, plankton scattering
- Drag to reorder tasks
- Pomodoro-style work/rest cycles with continuous mode
- Day timeline with slack and bedtime
- Grace system (mercy coins) for difficult moments
- Progressive disclosure — simple on day one, features emerge gradually
- Import/export backup
- Battery saver mode
- Noodle theme (unlockable)

---

## Running it

Open `boil.html` in a browser.

That's it.

For mobile, serve it statically and add to home screen.

---

## Philosophy

One file.

No server.
No tracking.
No accounts.

Your data stays in your browser.

If you clear it, it's gone — like steam.

---

## Design principles

- One task at a time
- Continuous feedback over discrete metrics
- Recovery over perfection
- Subtract before adding
- The water is the product
- Warm, not cool. Physical, not digital.
- Demanding, not punishing.
- Every feature earns its place or gets boiled off.

---

## Non-goals

- maximizing engagement
- maximizing streaks
- maximizing features
- becoming a productivity dashboard

---

## Final note

This is not a system to manage you.

It is a system to help you **stay with your work**.
